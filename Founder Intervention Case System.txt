Founder Intervention Case System (FICS)

Internal Build Brief for Team

Purpose

The Founder Intervention Case System (FICS) is the internal operating system for all founder intervention cases.

It is built to do 5 things:
	1.	create a consistent case structure from day one
	2.	track progress weekly in a measurable way
	3.	create comparable data across cases
	4.	document intervention work as proof
	5.	turn real interventions into case studies, insights, and intellectual property

This is not a simple intake form.
This is a repeatable case documentation system.

Every case must follow the same structure.

⸻

Core System Structure

Every case contains 4 fixed layers:
	1.	Case Profile
	2.	Weekly Intervention Log
	3.	Monthly Case Review
	4.	Case Outcome Report

Each case must move through these 4 layers in the same order.

This ensures consistency, comparability, and clean reporting.

⸻

1. CASE PROFILE

One-time setup at the start of the case

The Case Profile is the opening file of the intervention.
It captures the fixed information of the case.

This section will usually not change much after it is created.

Required fields

	•	Case ID
Example: LP-2026-01
	•	Case Status
Options:
	•	Active
	•	On Hold
	•	Completed
	•	Closed

Company Information
	•	Company Name
	•	Industry / Sector
	•	Country
	•	Revenue Range
	•	Number of Employees
	•	Ownership Structure
	•	Key Decision Makers

Founder Information
	•	Founder Name
	•	Founder Role
	•	Founder Age
	•	Founder Contact Details
	•	Main Internal Stakeholders

Intervention Setup
	•	Intervention Start Date
	•	Expected Intervention Duration
Options:
	•	3 months
	•	6 months
	•	9 months
	•	Primary Objective
Examples:
	•	Leadership reset
	•	Founder recovery
	•	Conflict resolution
	•	Exit preparation
	•	Strategic repositioning
	•	Organizational stabilization

Starting Situation
	•	Brief Case Summary
	•	Main Pressure Point
	•	Main Leadership Problem
	•	Main Business Risk
	•	Desired Outcome

Team instruction

This section must be completed before the first official weekly log starts.

2. WEEKLY INTERVENTION LOG

Main tracking layer of the system

This is the most important part of the entire system.

The Weekly Intervention Log creates comparable data over time.
Every active case must be updated once per week using the same categories and scoring logic.

This is how progress becomes visible.

Weekly Log Fields

Basic Weekly Data
	•	Week Number
	•	Date
	•	Session Duration
	•	Session Type
Options:
	•	Founder session
	•	Leadership session
	•	Strategic review
	•	Conflict intervention
	•	Follow-up call
	•	On-site intervention

⸻

A. Founder State

This measures the internal condition of the founder.

Required scores
	•	Energy: 1–10
	•	Clarity: 1–10
	•	Decision Power: 1–10

Required note
	•	Short Note
Maximum: 2 sentences

Scoring guidance
	•	1–3 = founder is under severe pressure
	•	4–6 = unstable but functional
	•	7–8 = stable and effective
	•	9–10 = highly clear, strong, and in control

⸻

B. Leadership Alignment

This measures how well the leadership structure is working together.

Required score
	•	Leadership Alignment: 1–10

Required note
	•	Short Note
Maximum: 2 sentences

Supporting prompt
	•	How aligned is the leadership team this week?

Scoring guidance
	•	1–3 = major dysfunction or distrust
	•	4–6 = partial alignment, recurring friction
	•	7–8 = mostly aligned with manageable tension
	•	9–10 = strong alignment and clear leadership cooperation

⸻

C. Decision Velocity

This measures the speed and quality of important decision-making.

Required score
	•	Decision Velocity: 1–10

Required notes
	•	Short Note
	•	What important decision was made this week?
	•	What important decision is still being avoided?

Scoring guidance
	•	1–3 = paralysis or major delays
	•	4–6 = decisions are slow and reactive
	•	7–8 = decisions are being made with reasonable speed
	•	9–10 = fast, clear, and disciplined decision-making

⸻

D. Structural Clarity

This measures whether roles, responsibilities, and authority are clear.

Required score
	•	Structural Clarity: 1–10

Required note
	•	Short Note

Supporting prompt
	•	Is it clear who is responsible for what?

Scoring guidance
	•	1–3 = major chaos, overlap, unclear authority
	•	4–6 = partial clarity, still dependent on founder
	•	7–8 = responsibilities are mostly clear
	•	9–10 = strong structure with clear accountability

⸻

E. Financial Awareness

This measures whether the founder understands the financial reality of the business.

Required score
	•	Financial Awareness: 1–10

Required note
	•	Short Note

Supporting prompt
	•	Does the founder have a realistic understanding of financial pressure, cash, and risk?

Scoring guidance
	•	1–3 = poor visibility and low awareness
	•	4–6 = partial understanding, some blind spots
	•	7–8 = generally strong awareness
	•	9–10 = clear understanding of financial reality and implications

⸻

F. Strategic Direction

This measures how clear the direction of the company is.

Required score
	•	Strategic Direction: 1–10

Required note
	•	Short Note

Supporting prompt
	•	Is the company operating with strategic clarity or reacting to pressure?

Scoring guidance
	•	1–3 = no clear direction
	•	4–6 = direction exists but is unstable or inconsistent
	•	7–8 = clear direction with some adjustment needed
	•	9–10 = strong strategic clarity and controlled execution

⸻

G. Founder Pressure Source

This identifies the dominant pressure source of the week.

Select one primary source
	•	Team conflict
	•	Growth pressure
	•	Financial pressure
	•	Legal pressure
	•	Personal stress

Optional
	•	Select one secondary pressure source if needed

⸻

H. Intervention Actions

This documents what was actually done during the week.

Required field
	•	Intervention Actions Taken

Examples
	•	Leadership alignment session
	•	Strategic restructuring
	•	Conflict mediation
	•	Decision forcing
	•	Founder repositioning
	•	Exit planning
	•	Role clarification
	•	Pressure mapping
	•	Stakeholder analysis

Required rule

This section must describe concrete intervention work.
Do not use vague language.

Bad example:
	•	supported founder

Strong example:
	•	facilitated leadership alignment session between founder and managing director
	•	redefined decision rights for weekly executive team
	•	identified 3 blocked strategic decisions and forced timeline for resolution

⸻

3. MONTHLY CASE REVIEW

Summary layer every 4 weeks

A Monthly Case Review must be completed after every 4 weekly logs.

The purpose is to step back from the weekly detail and identify the bigger movement in the case.

Required fields
	•	Month
	•	Biggest Breakthrough
	•	Biggest Risk
	•	Leadership Situation
	•	Founder State
	•	Next Intervention Focus

Team instruction

This review must be short, clear, and strategic.
It should summarize the pattern of the month, not repeat every weekly detail.

⸻

4. CASE OUTCOME REPORT

Closing report when the intervention ends

This report is created when the intervention is completed, paused, or formally closed.

The purpose is to capture the case outcome in a way that can later be used for:
	•	internal analysis
	•	strategic review
	•	anonymous case studies
	•	website case material
	•	intellectual property development

Required fields
	•	Start Situation
	•	Main Intervention
	•	Key Structural Changes
	•	Measured Progress
	•	Founder Outcome
	•	Company Outcome
	•	Future Outlook

Team instruction

This report must be written in a professional, factual style.
No exaggerated claims.
No coaching language.
It must reflect what changed in reality.

⸻

FOUNDER PRESSURE INDEX (FPI)

Core internal metric of the system

The Founder Pressure Index is the central tracking score inside FICS.

It creates one simple performance signal across the case.

Formula

The FPI is the average of these 4 weekly scores:
	•	Energy
	•	Decision Power
	•	Leadership Alignment
	•	Strategic Direction

Formula

FPI =
(Energy + Decision Power + Leadership Alignment + Strategic Direction) ÷ 4

Score interpretation
	•	8–10 = Strong leadership control
	•	5–7 = Unstable leadership zone
	•	1–4 = Founder crisis zone

Why this matters

This becomes our own internal framework for identifying pressure and tracking movement over time.

Over multiple cases, this will help us identify patterns such as:
	•	when founders lose control
	•	what pressure sources drive breakdown
	•	what intervention actions create the fastest recovery
	•	how long recovery or structural stabilization usually takes

SYSTEM RULES FOR THE TEAM

Rule 1

Every case must start with a complete Case Profile.

Rule 2

Every active case must have one Weekly Intervention Log per week.

Rule 3

Every 4 weeks, a Monthly Case Review must be added.

Rule 4

Every closed or completed case must have a Case Outcome Report.

Rule 5

The scoring system must remain consistent across all cases.

Rule 6

Notes must stay short, factual, and operational.

Rule 7

The system must be built so that it can later expand from founder-only tracking to company-level tracking.

That means the architecture should allow future add-ons such as:
	•	leadership team tracking
	•	stakeholder mapping
	•	conflict mapping
	•	exit readiness scoring
	•	financial risk scoring
